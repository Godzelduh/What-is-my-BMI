What is BMI?

    What is BMI?

  • Body Mass Index (BMI) is a widely used method to assess whether a person has a healthy body weight relative to their height. It is calculated using the formula:
  • BMI = Weight (kg) / Height (m)2 --or-- BMI = (Weight (lbs.) / Height (in)2) x 703.

    • BMI Categories:

    • Underweight: BMI less than 18.5
    • Normal weight: BMI 18.5 – 24.9
    • Overweight: BMI 25.0 – 29.9
    • Obesity: BMI 30.0 and above

    Facts about BMI:

    • BMI (Body Mass Index) is a tool that helps prevent health diseases by assessing weight in relation to height.
    • BMI has been in use since the 1930s and 1940s, and despite the emergence of various health metrics, it remains widely prevalent.
    • BMI calculates your health status by measuring your height and weight, providing insights to guide your next steps on your health journey.
    • While BMI is a valuable metric, remember that it should be used alongside other health assessments for a complete picture such as a calorie counter tool to track your weekly calorie intake.

    Relevance to Health:

    BMI is widely recognized as a quick and easy screening tool for potential weight-related health issues, such as heart disease, diabetes, and hypertension. However, it does not directly measure body fat or consider factors like muscle mass, bone density, and fat distribution. Therefore, while BMI provides a general indication of body fat, it should be complemented with other assessments to get a complete understanding of an individual's health status.
